## Project Overview
This project analyzes retail sales data to identify sales trends, profitability drivers, and business opportunities.

## Business Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze retail sales performance and identify factors influencing revenue and profitability. The analysis aims to support data-driven decision making related to pricing, discount strategies, product management, and regional sales performance.

### Correlation Analysis

<img width="493" height="447" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/aa8c47c4-9a89-4bb5-acea-21e4bf8683ff" />

A correlation analysis was conducted to understand the relationships among Sales, Quantity, Discount, and Profit. The results indicate a moderate positive relationship between Sales and Profit (0.48), suggesting that increased sales generally contribute to higher profitability. Discounts showed a negative correlation with Profit (-0.22), highlighting the potential impact of aggressive discounting on margins. Quantity exhibited only a weak relationship with Profit (0.07), indicating that higher sales volume alone does not guarantee improved profitability.

## Insights
- Technology generated the highest sales revenue among all categories.
- The West region contributed the largest share of total sales.
- California emerged as the highest revenue-generating state.
- Profitability decreases as discount levels increase.
- Sales and Profit show a moderate positive correlation (0.48).
- Sales peak during November and December, indicating strong seasonal demand.

## Recommendations
- Review high-discount product categories to improve profit margins.
- Focus marketing efforts on high-performing regions such as the West.
- Increase investment in Technology products due to strong and consistent sales performance.
- Leverage seasonal demand during November and December through targeted promotions and inventory planning.
- Monitor discount strategies to balance revenue growth with profitability.
